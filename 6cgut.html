üîπ Theories of business ethics (frameworks)

üìñ Utilitarianism (Bentham, Mill)

Greatest good for greatest number. Business decisions maximise overall happiness. e.g. CSR, sustainable practices. Downside: may harm minority.

üìñ Deontological ethics (Kant)

Duty, rules over consequences. Follow ethical rules (honesty, fairness) regardless of profit. No false advertising, no exploitation even if profitable.

üìñ Virtue ethics (Aristotle)

Character & virtues. What would a virtuous person do? Cultivate honesty, courage, compassion. Ethical culture & integrity.

üìñ Ethical relativism

No absolute truth; depends on cultural context. Multinationals adapt to local norms ‚Äî risk of blurring bribery/gift-giving.

üìñ Social contract theory (Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau)

Implied contract with society: business receives infrastructure/workforce, must act in public interest. Breach ‚Üí loss of licence to operate.

üìñ Care ethics

Prioritise relationships, empathy, vulnerable individuals. In business: employee well‚Äëbeing, community support, nurturing.
